Surprising Reasons for Oslo’s Home-Price Boom

House prices in the Norwegian capital are among the world’s highest, as measured by the average cost of a home relative to household median income. Nationwide, the cost of a home relative to income has almost doubled since the mid-1990s. Strong demand for housing in Norway has been driven by growing incomes, increasing population in the capital, and more and more immigrants.

It it notebaly to see that the price-to-income ratios have risen faster in Norway than in OECD (The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) and euro area countries.

In the meantime, it is reported that by 2040 half of Oslo’s population will have immigration background, refer Half of Oslo dwellers immigrants by 2040, quite a lot of blames are towards immigrants what who are the root cause for the increasing house price in the Norwegian capital.

Based on the statistics given by Statistics Norway from 1999 to 2015, in this article we will try to analysis which factor has the highest weight for the Oslo’s home-price boom, own population increase, immigration or growing incomes.This article uses Pearson correlation coefficient to determine the correlation weight, this means highest coefficient between two variable indicates highest correlation.

The first step is trying to use Python to fetch the raw data from ssb.no, for this analysis we should collect the population data, prices per square meter for used and new detached houses, average income.

It can be clearly to see that population boom has the highest Pearson correlation coefficient with both new and used house price per square meter, average gross income runs the second while immigration has the lowest correlation coefficient. this indicates that the immigration is not the most important reason for Oslo’s house-price boom, instead the total population boom is the major reason.
